# Asset Guide

## 1. Asset Mood

Assets should support a warm senior high school memory feeling:

- Nostalgic
- Soft
- Personal
- Handmade
- Golden
- Emotional
- Scrapbook-like

Avoid assets that feel futuristic, glossy, cyberpunk, overly commercial, or too abstract.

## 2. Photo Assets

Photos are the emotional center of the album.

Recommended photo themes:

- Classroom moments
- School hallway scenes
- Graduation or senior-year moments
- Desks, notebooks, blackboards, windows, and uniforms
- Friends, campus walks, sports fields, clubs, and after-school scenes
- Warm sunlight, film grain, and candid compositions

Recommended preparation:

- Use high-quality images with enough resolution for enlarged viewing.
- Prefer warm color grading and natural contrast.
- Keep faces and important subjects away from extreme edges.
- Prepare meaningful alt text for each image.
- Avoid overly dark, blurry, or generic stock-photo-looking images.

Suggested image sizes:

- Card thumbnail source: at least 800px wide
- Opened photo source: 1600px wide or larger when possible
- Use compressed web formats later, such as `.jpg`, `.webp`, or optimized `.png`

## 3. Card Front Assets

The collectible card front can use:

- Paper texture
- Small memory label
- Card number
- Date stamp
- Film strip fragment
- Pressed flower corner
- Tape piece
- Handwritten title area

The front side should make the photo feel hidden and collectible, like a memory waiting to be opened.

## 4. Decorative Assets

Useful decorative elements:

- Paper grain overlay
- Soft vignette
- Film strips
- Dried flowers
- Washi tape
- Notebook paper
- Torn paper edges
- Small school-themed stamps
- Handwritten doodles
- Subtle dust or film grain

Decorations should support the photos, not compete with them.

## 5. Background Assets

The background should feel like a warm scrapbook table or memory board.

Good background directions:

- Warm paper surface
- Soft golden window light
- Layered paper sheets
- Faint floral pattern
- Gentle film grain
- Slight blur or depth during opened photo state

Avoid strong gradients, neon glows, busy patterns, and high-contrast visual noise.

## 6. Suggested Folder Structure For Later

When implementation begins, assets can be organized like this:

```text
assets/
  photos/
    memories/
    thumbnails/
  textures/
    paper/
    film/
  decorations/
    flowers/
    tape/
    stamps/
  icons/
```

This structure is only a suggestion for the future build. It does not need to be created during the planning stage.

## 7. Naming Conventions

Use clear, consistent names:

```text
memory-classroom-window-01.jpg
memory-graduation-friends-01.jpg
texture-warm-paper-01.jpg
decor-flower-corner-rose-01.png
decor-film-strip-01.png
icon-like.svg
icon-comment.svg
```

## 8. Privacy And Permissions

Because this project may use real school photos:

- Confirm permission before using recognizable people.
- Avoid publishing private student information.
- Remove sensitive metadata from photos before public deployment.
- Use placeholder images during early development if real photos are not ready.

## 9. Placeholder Strategy

Before final photos are available, use placeholders that still match the mood:

- Warm campus photos
- Empty classroom photos
- Notebook and desk photos
- School corridor photos
- Film-style abstract memory photos

Do not use random generic placeholders that break the nostalgic school-album atmosphere.
